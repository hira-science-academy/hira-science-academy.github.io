Key Concepts: This foundational chapter establishes the number system framework through classification of numbers into rational and irrational numbers. Important Formulas:
‚Ä¢ \(\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}\)
‚Ä¢ \(\sqrt{\dfrac{a}{b}} = \dfrac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}\)

‚Ä¢ Additive Identity: \(a + 0 = a\)
‚Ä¢ Multiplicative Identity: \(a \times 1 = a\)

Key Concepts: This chapter introduces logarithms as the inverse of exponents. It covers the fundamental laws of logarithms, common logarithms (base 10), and their applications in simplifying calculations. The relationship between exponents and logarithms is explored through various examples and problems.

Laws of Logarithms:
‚Ä¢ \(\log_a(mn) = \log_a m + \log_a n\)
‚Ä¢ \(\log_a\left(\frac{m}{n}\right) = \log_a m - \log_a n\)
‚Ä¢ \(\log_a(m^n) = n \log_a m\)
‚Ä¢ \(\log_a a = 1\)
‚Ä¢ \(\log_a 1 = 0\)
‚Ä¢ Change of Base: \(\log_a b = \frac{\log_n b}{\log_n a}\)

üìí Chapter 6 ‚Äì Trigonometry ‚ûï

Key Concepts: This chapter introduces trigonometric ratios (sine, cosine, tangent) for acute angles in right-angled triangles. It covers fundamental trigonometric identities, applications to solve right triangles, and angle measurement in degrees. The chapter also includes practical applications of trigonometry in real-world problems.

Trigonometric Ratios:
‚Ä¢ \(\sin\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
‚Ä¢ \(\cos\theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
‚Ä¢ \(\tan\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}\)
‚Ä¢ \(\csc\theta = \frac{1}{\sin\theta}\)
‚Ä¢ \(\sec\theta = \frac{1}{\cos\theta}\)
‚Ä¢ \(\cot\theta = \frac{1}{\tan\theta}\)
Fundamental Identities:
‚Ä¢ \(\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1\)
‚Ä¢ \(1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta\)
‚Ä¢ \(1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta\)
‚Ä¢ \(\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\)
‚Ä¢ \(\sin(90^\circ - \theta) = \cos\theta\)
‚Ä¢ \(\cos(90^\circ - \theta) = \sin\theta\)

Key Concepts: This chapter introduces the Cartesian coordinate system and covers fundamental concepts including distance between points, midpoint formula, slope of a line, and graphing linear equations. It includes applications of coordinate geometry in solving geometric problems.

üìô Chapter 9 ‚Äì Similar Figures ‚ûï

Key Concepts: This chapter covers the concept of similarity in geometric figures, including criteria for similarity of triangles (AA, SAS, SSS), properties of similar figures, and applications of similarity in solving problems. It includes the relationship between ratios of corresponding sides, areas, and volumes.

Similarity Criteria for Triangles:
‚Ä¢ AA (Angle-Angle): Two corresponding angles equal \( (\angle A = \angle P, \angle B = \angle Q) \)
‚Ä¢ SAS (Side-Angle-Side): Proportional sides and included angle equal \( \left(\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{AC}{PR}, \angle A = \angle P\right) \)
‚Ä¢ SSS (Side-Side-Side): All corresponding sides proportional \( \left(\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BC}{QR} = \frac{AC}{PR}\right) \)
Properties of Similar Figures:
‚Ä¢ Corresponding angles are equal
‚Ä¢ Corresponding sides are proportional
‚Ä¢ Ratio of areas = (Ratio of sides)¬≤
‚Ä¢ Ratio of volumes = (Ratio of sides)¬≥ (for 3D figures)
‚Ä¢ Perimeters are proportional to corresponding sides

üìô Chapter 13 ‚Äì Probability ‚ûï

Key Concepts: This chapter introduces basic probability concepts including sample space, events, and calculation of probabilities for simple events. It covers theoretical probability, experimental probability, and the relationship between them. The chapter includes probability of complementary events and applications to simple real-world situations.

Probability Fundamentals:
‚Ä¢ Theoretical Probability: \( P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total possible outcomes}} \)
‚Ä¢ Experimental Probability: \( \frac{\text{Number of times event occurs}}{\text{Total trials}} \)
‚Ä¢ Probability Range: \( 0 \leq P(E) \leq 1 \)
‚Ä¢ Complementary Events: \( P(\text{not } E) = 1 - P(E) \)
‚Ä¢ Equally Likely Outcomes: \( P(E) = \frac{n(E)}{n(S)} \)
