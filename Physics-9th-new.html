Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between physical and non-physical quantities?

Physical quantities can be measured (like length, mass, time) while non-physical quantities cannot be measured with instruments (like love, fear). Physical quantities have units and dimensions, non-physical quantities don't.

How do I calculate least count of a vernier caliper?

Least count = Value of 1 main scale division (MSD) - Value of 1 vernier scale division (VSD). Typically for a standard vernier caliper: LC = 1 mm - 0.9 mm = 0.1 mm or 0.01 cm.

What are the three equations of motion?

The three equations are:
1. v = u + at
2. s = ut + ¬Ωat¬≤
3. 2as = v¬≤ - u¬≤
Where v=final velocity, u=initial velocity, a=acceleration, s=distance, t=time.

What's the difference between mass and weight?

Mass is the quantity of matter (measured in kg), while weight is the force of gravity on that mass (measured in N). Mass is constant, weight changes with gravity.

How does an electromagnet work?

An electromagnet works by passing electric current through a coil of wire wound around an iron core. The current creates a magnetic field that magnetizes the iron core, creating a strong magnet. The magnetism disappears when the current is turned off.
